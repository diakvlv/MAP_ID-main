Sloveso “být”. Rody podst. jmen

(Verb “to be”. Grammar genders)

SLOVESO BÝT

In Czech there must ALWAYS be some kind of active verb.
For example: I am Danil. She is tall. The lamp is white. They are from Czechia.
×

PRONOUNS

já - I
ty - you
my - we
vy - you (plural)
on - he
ona - she
ono - it
oni - they

Positive form of the verb - být

PRONUNCIATION OF FORMS OF THE VERB "být"
jsem - sem = I am
jsi - si = You are
jsme - sme = We are
jste - ste = You are
jsou - sou = They are

"J" IS NOT PRONOUNCED!

BUT
JE [ye] = he/she/it

ATTENTION!

já ≠ jsem
ty ≠ jsi
my ≠ jsme
vy ≠ jste
on ≠ je
ona ≠ je
ono ≠ je
oni ≠ jsou

JÁ - TY - MY - VY - ON - ONA - ONO - ONI - are INDEPENDENT pronouns

For example:

- I am Danil - Jsem Danil
- I am a boy - Jsem kluk
- I am tall - Jsem vysoký
- Where are you from? - Odkud jsi?
- Are you home? - Jsi doma?
- Are you at work? - Jsi v práci?
- Dáša is beautiful - Dáša je hezká
- The table is large - Stůl je velký
- My girlfriend is Czech - Moje přítelkyně je Češka
- Window is white - Okno je bílé
- We are from Czechia - Jsme z Česka
- We are from Germany - Jsme z Německa
- We are foreigners (We are foreigners) - Jsme cizinci - Sme cizinci
- We are at home (We are at home) - Jsme doma - Sme at home
- Who are you? (Who are you?) - Kdo jste? - Where ste?
- Where are you from? (where are you from?) - Odkud jste? -Where are you from?
- Are you Peter? - Jste Petr?
- They are from Kiev - Jsou z Kyjeva
- Are they at home? - Jsou doma?
- Why are they at work? - Proč jsou v práci?

Negative form of the verb - být

For example:

- I am not Tanya - Nejsem Tanja
- I am not Czech, I am Russian - Nejsem Češka, jsem Ruska
- I'm not at home - Nejsem doma
- Why are not you at work? - Proč nejsi v práci?
- Why aren't you at home? - Proč nejsi doma?
- Are you not a foreigner? - Nejsi cizinec?
- Aren't you Peter? - Nejsi Petr?
- David is not ugly - David není ošklivý

- Window is not black - Okno není černé

- Why aren't you at work? - Proč nejste v práci?

Make sure that it does not depend on word order in negative sentences!

Neutrum - střední rod

NEUTURUM ENDS WITH:
- O

For example:

Okno - [ocno] (translation: window)
Auto - [a u to] (translation: auto)
Kolo - [colo] (translation: bicycle)
Břicho - [brzhiho] (translation: belly)

- E

For example:

Moře - [morzhe] (translation: sea)
Srdce - [srdtse] (translation: heart)
Nebe - [nebe] (translation: sky)
Pole - [po le] (translation: field)

- Í

For example:

Náměstí - [naamnesti] (translation: area)
Nádraží - [naadrazhi] (translation: station)
Svědomí - [swedomii] (translation: conscience)
Nábřeží - [naabrzhezhii] (translation: embankment)

Femininum - ženský rod

FEMININUM ENDS WITH:
- A

For example:

Zena - (transaltion: wife)
Kniha - (translation: book)
Lampa - (translation: lamp)
Kabelka - (translation: bag)

- E

For example:

Moře - [morzhe] (translation: sea)
Srdce - [srdtse] (translation: heart)
Nebe - [nebe] (translation: sky)
Pole - [po le] (translation: field)

How to distinguish from neuter gender?
In most cases (83% of Czech words) they are femininum. And if it ends with CE, then it’s definitely femininum.

For example:

Židle - [zhidle] (translation: chair)
Práce - [praatse] (translation: work)
Učebnice - [oochebneetse] (translation: textbook)
Ulice - [ooleetse] (translation: street)

!!Měkká souhláska - ž š č ř ď ť ň c j (soft consonants)

For example:

Kancelář - [cantselaarzh] (translation: office)
Pláž - [plaazh] (translation: beach)
Garáž - [garraazh] (translation: garage)
Tramvaj - [tramvay] (translation: tram)

- OST

For example:

Kost - [cost] (translation: bone)
Místnost - [meestnost] (translation: room)
Výslovnost - [veeslovnost] (translation: pronunciation)
Společnost - [spolechnost] (translation: company (business))

Masculinum - mužský rod

THE MAIN INDICATOR OF THE MASCULINUM GENDER IS CONSONANT
HARD CONSONANTS - g h ch k r d t n

For example:

Krok - [croc] (translation: step)
Grog - [grog] (translation: grog)
Hoch - [ghoh] (translation: guy)
Prst - [p r s t] (translation: company (finger))

SOFT CONSONANTS - ž š č ř ď ť ň c j

For example:

Diář - [deearzh] (translation: diary)
Čaj - [chay] (translation: tea)
Sleď - [sled] (translation: herring)
Dešť - [desht] (translation: rain)

Adjectives, pronouns and the number 1

PRONOUN THIS - TEN, THIS - TA, THIS - TO


MASCULINUM
TEN JEDEN - THIS (masculinum) ONE
JAKÝ? - WHICH (masculinum) ONE

FEMININUM
TA JEDNA - THIS (femininum) ONE
JAKÁ? - WHICH (femininum) ONE

NEUTURUM
TO JEDNO - THIS (neutrum) ONE
JAKÉ? - WHICH (neutrum) ONE

FIRST TYPE OF ADJECTIVES

When we talk about adjectives, the ending of the adjective changes according to gender.
Masculinum: new - (jaký - which) - nový
Femininum: new - (jaká - which) - nová
Neutrum: new - (jaké - which) - nové

SECOND TYPE OF ADJECTIVES

If the ending of the adjective is a long soft - Í, then we do not change the ending.
Masculinum: foreign - (jaký - which) - cizí
Femininum: foreign - (jaká - which) - cizí
Neutrum: foreign - (jaké - which) - cizí
