1. Česká abeceda.

Czech alphabet.

Let's watch the video first!

The Czech alphabet is based on the Latin alphabet with the addition of letters that are unusual for the Latin alphabet.
There are 42 letters in the Czech language.
An important feature of the Czech language is that words and letters in it are read in the same way as they are written.

Watch the video carefully. It explains how to read Czech letters correctly and how to practice their pronunciation.
"A" - strong A - ano (yes)
"Á" - long AA - mám (I have)
"B" - as English B - banka (bank)
"C" - TS - citron (lemon)
“Č” - CH as in China - čaj (tea)
"D" - D as in Denmark - deník (diary)
“Ď” - soft D
“E” - E as in elbow - metr (meter)
“É” - longer E - problém (problem)
“Ě” - soft E - těší mě [tyeshii mnye] - nice to meat you
“F” - F - kafe (coffee)
“G” - G as in geoGraphy - garáž (garage)
“H” - “GH” - as Ukrainian [Г] or Greek [Γ γ] - historie [ghistorie] (history), ahoj [aghoy] (hello)
“Ch” - read as strong H or Greek [Х] - chléb (bread)
"I" - short I as in Italy
“Í” - long I
“J” - Y in [yes], ještě [yeshtye] (more)
“K” - K - koruna (crown)
“L” - L, the tongue goes back a little
“M” - M - muzeum (museum)
"N" - hard N
"Ň" - soft N
“O” - O as in bow - slovo (word)
“Ó” - long O - gól (goal)
“P” - P - Praha [Pragha] (Prague)
"Q" - "kve" - IQ [EE kwe]
"R" - R as in Spanish
“Ř” - RZh or RSh - řeka [rzheka] (river), přišel [prshishel] (came), třída [trshchiida] (class)
"S" - S
“Š” - Sh - škola (school)
“T” - T - tram
“Ť” - soft Т
“U” - U as in cool - kultura [cooltoora] (culture)
“Ú” - long U - úsměv [oosmnev] (smile), úkol [ookol] (task)
“Ů” - půjdu [pooydu]
“V” - V - vidím (I see)
"W" - W, not often used in Czech words
“X” - X
“Y” - We analyze the difference with “I” in the video
“Ý” - We analyze the difference with “Í” in the video
“Z” - Z
“Ž” - Zh - židle [zhidle] (chair)

!EXCEPTION!
When reading words where the letter ě comes after the letter m, the sound n will be added.
For example, the word město (city) will be read as mnesto.
Word náměstí (square) will be read as naamnesti.
"Háček" is translated as "hook". The letter changes completely and is pronounced differently, usually softly. "Čárka" is translated as "comma". Affects the length of pronunciation.
